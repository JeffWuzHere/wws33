# Knowledge Base Entry: reCAPTCHA Not Displaying on Wholesale Lead Registration Form Due to WPForms No-Conflict Mode

## 🎯 Executive Summary
**The Issue:** The reCAPTCHA widget on the Wholesale Lead Capture registration form fails to render (the `#recaptcha_field` div is present in the HTML but invisible), causing a security validation error that prevents customers from submitting the form — despite valid reCAPTCHA keys working on other forms on the same site.
**The Fix:** Disable the **No-Conflict Mode** setting in WPForms (WPForms > Settings), which strips reCAPTCHA scripts from all non-WPForms contexts, including the Wholesale Lead registration form.

---

## 🛠 Final Resolution (The "2nd Me" Guide)
> [!TIP]
> **Use this solution if a customer asks about:** recaptcha not showing wholesale lead form, recaptcha missing registration form, wholesale lead form submission blocked, recaptcha works on other forms but not lead capture, recaptcha field invisible, wpforms conflict recaptcha
>
> **Steps to solve:**
> 1. Confirm reCAPTCHA keys are valid and working on other forms on the site (e.g., WPForms forms) — this rules out a key configuration issue.
> 2. Check whether WPForms is installed and active on the site.
> 3. In WordPress admin, go to **WPForms > Settings**.
> 4. Locate the **No-Conflict Mode** option and **disable** it.
> 5. Clear any caching plugins and server-side caches.
> 6. Test the Wholesale Lead registration form — the reCAPTCHA widget should now render and the form should be submittable.
>
> **Note:** If the customer needs to keep No-Conflict Mode enabled for other reasons, advise them to contact WPForms support to request an exclusion for pages containing the Wholesale Lead registration form.

## 💡 Internal Insights (If applicable)
- WPForms No-Conflict Mode is designed to prevent reCAPTCHA conflicts between multiple plugins but is overly aggressive — it strips reCAPTCHA scripts globally, not just from WPForms page contexts.
- The symptom is distinctive: the `#recaptcha_field` div is present in source but does not render visually, and the reCAPTCHA JS is absent from the page's loaded scripts.
- This is a third-party plugin conflict with no fix required on the Wholesale Suite side. The resolution is entirely within WPForms settings.
- If a customer cannot disable No-Conflict Mode (e.g., they rely on it for their WPForms setup), the alternative is to ask WPForms support for a whitelist/exclusion mechanism or consider using a different reCAPTCHA integration approach.
